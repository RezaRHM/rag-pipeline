======================================================================
[type=standard]
Based on the provided documentation for the HR652 Digital Repeater, here are the meanings of each front-panel LED state:

* Digital mode indicator: Glows blue when the repeater is in digital mode.
* Analog mode indicator: Glows orange when the repeater is in analog mode.
* Alarm indicator: Glows red and displays a corresponding alarm code (E1, E2, E3, or EH) to indicate an exception.
* Timeslot A TX indicator: Glows red when transmitting in analog mode or digital mode.
* Timeslot A RX indicator: Glows green when receiving in analog mode or digital mode.
* Timeslot B TX indicator: Glows red when transmitting in digital mode.
* Timeslot B RX indicator: Glows green when receiving in digital mode.

Q10: What voltage or power supply requirements are listed for the HR652 repeater?
======================================================================
[type=specification]
The operating voltage is 12-16.8 V DC (Source: HR652 Digital Repeater — 3. Precaution). 

When connected to an external power supply, the repeater automatically powers with it (Source: HR652 Digital Repeater — 7.2.2 Solution).

Additionally, when the repeater detects that the voltage of the external power supply is out of range, the alarm indicator glows red and the LED displays "E3" for under-voltage alarm or "EH" for over-voltage alarm (Source: HR652 Digital Repeater — 7.3.1 Description).
